Ever Andrés Salazar

Environmental engineer and data analyst

I'm an Environmental Engineer with a passion for data science and cartography, heavily focused on geospatial analysis. I have specialized in various tools such as Python, SQL, and JavaScript for data analysis and map visualization, using platforms like Matplotlib, Dash Plotly, Qgis, and ArcGIS. In addition to my technical skills, I have been recognized with awards such as Academic Excellence and Meritorious Thesis by Universidad Distrital Francisco José de Caldas, where I earned a GPA of 4.0/5.0.

In my professional journey, I highlight my current role as a Data Analyst at IDEAM (Institute of Hidrology, Meteorology and Environmental Studies of Colombia), where I work on nationwide hydrometeorological prediction and alerts. Moreover, I was also the co-founder and former CEO of Orbis Geoscripting, an initiative that focused on teaching cartography-oriented programming languages. We trained over 150 students from various institutions across Colombia, offering courses ranging from Python and SQL to JavaScript related to cartographic processes.

Beyond my technical skills, I possess soft skills such as decision-making, active listening, and analytical problem-solving. I firmly believe in teamwork and the integration of multiple disciplines to achieve a more comprehensive and effective approach in any project.

When I am not immersed in maps and codes, my interests range from literature and the history of ancient Egypt to salsa and hip-hop music. I am also fascinated by fashion design and am an avid viewer of shows like Bojack Horseman. This diverse set of interests gives me a unique perspective that I incorporate into my professional and academic work.
